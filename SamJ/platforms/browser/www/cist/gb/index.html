Contraception and STI

Contraception is a public health priority for teenagers.
The ideal for a safe sexuality: contraception and the condom (male or female). Contraception prevents pregnancy. The condom is the only way to protect against Sexually Transmitted Infections (STI).
Unfortunately, nearly one teenager out of two has her first sexual experiences without any contraception, running the risk of becoming pregnant.
It is vital for the teenager to get in touch with a doctor to determine the right contraceptive within the first consultation.
 As a priority, condoms and hormonal contraception are proposed to teenagers.
