Smoking

Smoking is the major cause of preventable death and the psychoactive substance which is the most common cause of deaths in accordance with the number of smokers. According to World Health Organization, about one person in the world dies every six seconds because of smoking. In France, smoking kills 78000 persons each year, as to say more than 200 persons per day.

1. Tobacco is the most addictive and the most harmful product.

2. It is the most consumed product by young people every day and their first addiction.

3. In spite of a drop in consumption since the beginning of 2000, the proportion of intensive young smokers has remained stable. It shows how difficult it is to modify the behaviour of the most tobacco-addicted adolescents.

4. For adolescents, smoking is combined to other addictions (alcohol, cannabis, etc.)

5. The more precocious smoking is, the more severe the addiction is

6. Occasional consumption is enough to cause addiction

7. The earlier you tackle smoking the easier you can stop

8. Underperforming at school is particularly connected to the daily tobacco consumption of young people

9. Tobacco is the only product for which levels of consumption are equivalent between girls and boys.

10. Secondary school is a period of intense initiation and a time conducive to regular consumption: moving up from Year 9 to the end of Year 10 is a transition period

11. Use of chicha is widespread among young people

12. Most of young people welcomed in CJC smoke, of whom 80% smoke daily
