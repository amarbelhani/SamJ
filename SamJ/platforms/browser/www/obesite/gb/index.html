Obesity

Obesity is a situation where a person has abnormally accumulated excessive body fat that could be harmful to health.
As teenagers, WE are the most affected people by obesity.

What’s the difference between obesity and overweight?

 Overweight is a condition when BMI ranges from 25 to 29.9
Overweight is a high concentration of body fat but it is not bad for your health.

 Obesity is a condition when BMI reaches or goes over 30
Obesity can be considered as chronic disease that can have negative effects on your daily life but on your health too.

Calculate your BMI: Body Mass Index

BMI is used to determine your corpulence, if you have a weight fitting to your height, if you are overweight or if you are obese.
Here are the different stages of obesity:

 Obese, BMI from 30 to 34.4
 Severely Obese, BMI from 35 to 39.9
 Morbidly Obese, BMI from 40 to 40.9
 Super Obese, BMI equal or superior to 50

Causes of obesity:

Food energy intake is obviously the most important cause of obesity.
- bad dietary habits : like « Fast Food », plentiful meals, high in sugar and fat
- genetics
- nervous depression or even stress can lead to obesity, because we eat to forget our problems.
-insufficient sleep : French adults aged from 18 to 55 sleep approximately 7 hours per night on weekdays, and more than 1 out of 3 only sleep 6 hours per night. Teenagers sleep less than 8 hours per night regarding half of us. But, it is recommended to sleep from 8h30 to 9h15 per night.

Consequences / Dangers:

Consequences linked to obesity can be psychological or physical.
Psychological factors: it is hard to make some everyday life gestures, discrimination can provoke uneasiness.
Physiques factors: cardiovascular diseases such as sleep apnoea and high blood pressure.
Treatment: hydrotherapy, medicine with psychological follow-ups and gastric surgery as a last resort.
