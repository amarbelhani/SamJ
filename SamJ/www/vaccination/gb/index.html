Vaccination

Vaccination is a way of protecting itself against the development of some diseases caused by microbes (bacteria or viruses), before this person is affected by these diseases. ... The microbe, rendered harmless, does not cause the disease.

In 2018, vaccinations against Diphtheria, Tetanus and Poliomyelitis, Pertussis, Haemophilus influenzae b, Hepatitis B, Meningococcus C, Pneumococcus, Measles, Mumps, Rubella are obligatory before the age of 2 years for infants born on or after January 1, 2018.

During adolescence, vaccinations remain necessary. These may be vaccinations useful at this time of life or catch-up for vaccinations not performed during childhood.

Recommendation:


Meningococcal meningitis and sepsis C: Vaccination up to and including 24 years.

Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Infections: For all unvaccinated 14-year-old girls. .

Hepatitis B: For unvaccinated adolescents, catch-up is possible up to 15 years.
