Alcohol

Alcohol is a psychoactive substance, that’s to say it has some negative effects on the brain: it can alter consciousness, perception and behaviour.

In France, 23 youngsters are killed on the road every year, roughly 1250 per year.
This is the main death rate for young people between the ages of 15 and 24 without considering thousands of seriously injured persons.

Alcohol slows down reflexes and reduces vigilance.

The risk of lethal accident increases very fast according to blood alcohol level: from 0.5 gram of alcohol per litre, the risk is multiplied by 2 then by 10 from 0.8 gram per litre and by 35 at 1.2 gram per litre.

A massive consumption of alcohol in a short period of time (which is sometimes defined as ‘Binge drinking’ or ‘cuite express’) has immediate effects on the body.

Drunkenness can have side effects such as nausea, vomiting, memory loss and euphoria. When drinking large quantities of alcohol, you can lose consciousness: that is ethylic coma, real poisoning which requires to be hospitalized in emergency. You may die out for lack of treatment.
